1920

1920–1940 – Interwar Period and the Rise of Modernism

In the 1920s, Mainz developed into a center for art, culture, and science. The city experienced a heyday of architecture in the New Objectivity and Bauhaus styles. The University of Mainz was founded in 1946, but preparations and planning had already begun in the 1920s. The economy slowly recovered, but the political situation worsened towards the end of the 1930s with the rise of National Socialism.

1940

1940–1960 – World War II and Reconstruction

During World War II, Mainz was hit hard by air raids, resulting in significant destruction. After the war in 1945, the city lay in ruins. The French occupying forces took over administration, and reconstruction began in the late 1940s. In the 1950s, Mainz experienced an economic boom, characterized by the expansion of infrastructure, the reconstruction of historic buildings, and the establishment of new businesses. The university was rebuilt and gained importance, making the city an important center of education and science.

In 1942, Anna Seghers wrote the novel "The 7th Cross." The book is set in Mainz during World War II, shortly before the city's liberation by the Allies. It tells the story of seven prisoners who escape from a Nazi concentration camp and attempt to find refuge in the city. It centers on the character of Georg Heisler, who is on the run from the Nazis. The novel depicts the fear, courage, and hope of people in a time of great danger. Anna Seghers describes moral decisions and human behavior in a dark era. The work is considered an important example of the literature of resistance against the Nazi regime.

The Seventh Cross (1944) is one of the films in which this novel was adapted into a film. Somewhere in Nazi Germany there is a concentration camp. And in that camp, there are seven crosses, one for each of the men who dared to escape. One by one, the Gestapo hunts the seven down, and returns them to the crosses. And death. Until there is but one cross left. And one man left trying to escape it. Fred Zinnemann (High Noon) directs Spencer Tracy at the head of a terrifically talented ensemble that includes such notables as Hume Cronyn and Jessica Tandy (their first appearance together), Ray Collins, Signe Hasso and Agnes Moorehead. This tense, terse and gripping war-time thriller both plumbs the depths of human depravity and soars to the heights of human charity and sacrifice, as one man's journey through hell restores his faith in mankind.

1960

1960–1980 – Economic Boom and Social Change

In the 1960s and 1970s, Mainz developed into an important media and communications center. The founding of ZDF (Second German Television) in 1961 brought the city national media prominence. The population grew, and the city expanded its infrastructure, including new residential areas and transport routes. The university continued to expand, and Mainz increasingly became a center for science, culture, and media.

1980

1980–2000 – Modernization and Cultural Diversity

In the last decades of the 20th century, Mainz focused on modernization. New districts emerged, and the old town was restored. The city became known for its vibrant cultural scene, the Mainz Carnival, and its wine tradition. The economy diversified, with a focus on media, science, and services. Mainz also became more internationally connected, with partnerships in various countries.

2000

2000–2020 – Digitalization, Sustainability, and Cultural Diversity

In the new millennium, Mainz has focused heavily on digitalization and sustainable urban development. The city has implemented modern infrastructure projects to make public transportation more environmentally friendly and is committed to renewable energies. Culturally, Mainz remains vibrant, with numerous festivals, museums, and events that reflect the city's diversity. The University of Mainz continues to grow and is an important center for research and innovation. Tourism is also gaining importance thanks to the historic old town, the cathedral, and the wine region. Mainz presents itself as a modern, cosmopolitan city that preserves its traditions while remaining future-oriented.

2020–2024 – Challenges, Innovations, and Social Change

Mainz faced various challenges between 2020 and 2024, particularly the global COVID-19 pandemic, which had a significant impact on social life, the economy, and healthcare. The city quickly took measures to protect its population, support local commerce, and advance digitalization. During this time, many events and cultural activities were digitized or adapted to strengthen the community. Mainz also increased its investments in sustainable projects to promote climate protection, for example, by expanding environmentally friendly means of transport and greening urban spaces. The university and local companies developed innovative solutions to make the economy more resilient. Overall, Mainz demonstrated strong resilience, innovative strength, and a desire to remain a livable, sustainable city for all residents.
