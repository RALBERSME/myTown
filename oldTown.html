Mainz's Old Town is the historic heart of the city and captivates visitors with its charming atmosphere. Located on the Rhine River, it is known for its narrow streets and old half-timbered houses.

A major landmark is Mainz Cathedral, built in the 10th century and dominating the cityscape. The Old Town is also home to the Gutenberg Museum, which showcases the history of book printing and Johannes Gutenberg, the inventor of the printing press.

The pedestrian zone in the Old Town is popular with shoppers and offers many cafés and restaurants. The Town Hall, built in the Baroque style, is also located in the Old Town and is an impressive building.

The Mainz Wine Market takes place here annually and attracts many visitors. The Old Town is also known for its historic fountains, such as the Schiller Fountain. Many of the buildings in the Old Town date back to the Middle Ages and have been lovingly restored.

Its proximity to the Rhine makes the Old Town an ideal place for strolls along the water. The Old Town also boasts numerous small museums and galleries that reflect the region's culture. The lively atmosphere is enhanced by numerous events and markets throughout the year. Overall, Mainz's Old Town is a fascinating place that combines history, culture, and joie de vivre.
